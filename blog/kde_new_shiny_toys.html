KDE: New shiny toys

Posted: Mon, 03 Oct 2011

Two new toys for you to try out: Bangarang and PublicTransport.

Bangarang

Bangarang is a relatively new media player based on key KDE technologies: Phonon for multimedia output and Nepomuk for semantic storage.

The player feels very snappy, and is like a breath of fresh air after Amarok 2.x. Don’t get me wrong, I love Amarok’s extended abilities. But sometimes you just want to listen to a few songs, and Bangarang seems perfect for that.

PublicTransport

is a very nice plasmoid. In fact, it’s the only one I can see myself using along with my old SmoothWebView plasmoid. It’s purpose: Showing you departing trains/busses/etc at a defined stop.

Its filtering system is very powerful:

  • You can filter departures easily via their route (called “color filter”)
  • You can specify own filters that filter via transport type, time, etc.
